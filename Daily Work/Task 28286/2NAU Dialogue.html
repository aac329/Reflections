Dialogue between a 2NAU Transition Coordinator and a 2NAU student:

TC: So I understand you’re having difficulty deciding on a major. I have a few resources that I think you might be interested in, which will help you narrow it down a little bit. But before I do that, I just want to talk with you to see if we can narrow down what your interests are, and maybe get an idea of what your career preferences are.

Student: Well I’m just not sure. I know I really like working with people. I like giving back to the community.

TC: What type of work environment do you think you see yourself working in? Office, teaching, maybe even outdoors?

Student: I think I’d like to work in an office climate that is truly making a difference.

TC: Okay, so are you thinking maybe social work, or working in the medical field? What does making a difference mean to you?

Student: I think if I really had to pick an ideal fit for me, it would be to work in the medical profession. But not necessarily a nurse or a doctor – I don’t really like needles – so more on the administrative side. Helping people to connect to the right resource

TC: Okay, I do have a degree program in mind in our health professions that I think might be a good fit for you. It’s a bachelor’s of science in health sciences with an emphasis in public health. It’s basically designed for people such as yourself who are interested in working in the medical area and health, but not necessarily as a practitioner in a certain field. It’ll prepare you for working in various roles such as behavioral health, nutrition, injury prevention, things like that. One of our graduates ended up pursuing a master of administration degree and got a job as a director for health promotions in Yavapai county. So not only will this degree in public health help you to pursue a career that you might enjoy, but also if in the future you’re interested in a graduate degree, it’ll help you go that way as well. Does this sound like something you might be interested in?

Student: This sounds really interesting. I’d like to learn more about this.

TC: Alright, let me go ahead and give you those resources such as O-NET and the Bureau of Labor and Statistic site, which will give you further insight as to the career opportunities available for working in public health.

Student: Okay, sounds good!
