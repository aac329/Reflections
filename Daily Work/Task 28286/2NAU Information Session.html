Welcome to the 2NAU Information Session


What is 2NAU?

2NAU is a Joint Admission program.

What does ‘Joint Admission’ mean?
That means you are admitted to NAU while you earn your associate degree at your community college.

What does that mean for me?
As a 2NAU student, you receive guidance throughout your educational career so that you stay on track to complete your bachelor’s degree at NAU.

Download our 2NAU Joint Admission guide


2NAU Joint Admission Benefits:

Affordable:

  1. While taking coursework at the community college, you pay the community college tuition rate.
  2. When you are ready to transition into your NAU bachelor’s degree program, you can take advantage of the most affordable university tuition rate in the state.
  3. As a 2NAU student, you may be eligible for a 2NAU merit-based tuition scholarship. If you qualify, you may receive $1,000 per term for your first two consecutive terms.

A Clear Path:

  1. NAU partners with a variety of Arizona community colleges to make it easier for you to complete your bachelor’s degree.
  2. This partnership will help you succeed at your local community college as well as at the University.
  3. NAU will provide you with guidance throughout the first two years and the support you need to finish a four-year degree.

NAU Advisor:

  1. You will receive personalized guidance and support from a 2NAU Transition Coordinator who is here to help you stay on track for a seamless transition into your bachelor’s degree program.
  2. Read a typical dialogue between a 2NAU Transition Coordinator and a 2NAU student.

Early Enrollment:

  1. When you’re ready to enroll in classes for your NAU bachelor’s degree program, you will have access to early enrollment. That means you avoid the “rush” and get the classes you want, when you want them.
  2. NAU offers many degree programs locally, online or in Flagstaff.

You're a Lumberjack!
As a 2NAU participant, you will receive:

  1. An official NAU student ID card at no charge
  2. A personal nau.edu email account
  3. Access to our award-winning university research library
  4. A monthly newsletter with college success tips and insider updates
  5. Access to NAU athletic events in Flagstaff

2NAU is best for students who are:

  1. In their first or second community college semester at one of these participating community colleges
  2. Committed to completing their bachelor’s degree:
    1. Choose from one of over 30 statewide NAU-Extended Campuses to complete your bachelor’s degree.
    2. All of the bachelor’s programs available at NAU-Extended Campuses are designed to be transfer programs.
    3. Learn more about our innovative and accredited bachelor’s degree programs.

Do you qualify? Read the following FAQs to learn more:

  1. What if I’m in my third or fourth semester?
    1. If you are in your third or fourth community college semester or have already completed all of your community college credits, your local Transition Coordinator can help you explore the best NAU transfer option for you.
  2. Are there other transfer options?
    1. Contact your local Transition Coordinator to help you determine whether 2NAU or a transfer option would be best for you.
  3. What if I’m ready to transfer now?
    1. If you plan to begin NAU courses immediately, you can apply to our transfer program.
  4. How and when should I apply?
    1. If you have determined that you are eligible for the 2NAU program, you should apply as soon as possible. Our free application is short and simple.
  5. Where’s the application?
    1. Start filling out the online 2NAU application here.

Self-test:

Question 1:

What are the requirements to qualify as a 2NAU student?

  1. You must be in your first or second semester at a community college.
  2. You must be committed to completing a bachelor’s degree.
  3. All of the above.

If you answered “C: All of the above,” you’re right! You must not only be in your first or second semester of a participating community college, but you should also be committed to completing a bachelor’s degree of your choice at NAU.

Question 2:

For those who don’t qualify for the 2NAU Joint Admission program, what is another option to pursue a bachelor’s degree at NAU?

  1. Apply to 2NAU anyway and see what happens.
  2. Apply to the NAU Transfer Program.
  3. Both A and B.

If you answered “B: Apply to the NAU Transfer Program,” you’re right! If you don’t qualify for 2NAU, we invite you to apply to our transfer program. Visit the NAU “Apply Now” web page to learn more.
